## Safety Is a Community Problem

In many Nigerian communities, safety challenges are not addressed by any single institution alone. They require coordinated effort — between residents, community leaders, local authorities, and support services. Yet the tools to enable this coordination have historically been unavailable to ordinary people.

**meSafe** is our answer to this challenge.

## What Is meSafe?

meSafe is a community safety platform developed under Orao Impact Foundation's **Safe Communities Initiative**. It is designed to:

- **Enable anonymous incident reporting** — allowing residents to flag safety concerns without fear.
- **Connect community responders** — linking verified community safety volunteers with those who need help.
- **Build situational awareness** — giving community leaders real-time visibility of safety patterns.
- **Foster trust** — by creating transparent feedback loops between reporters and responders.

> "When communities have the tools to communicate, they become far more resilient." — meSafe Programme Lead

## The Problem We Are Solving

Traditional emergency reporting systems face several challenges in underserved communities:

- **Limited trust** in formal authorities.
- **Poor connectivity** in rural and peri-urban areas.
- **No feedback mechanism** — reports disappear into a void.
- **Language and literacy barriers** that exclude large segments of the population.

meSafe is built with these realities in mind. It works on low-bandwidth connections, supports multiple local languages, and prioritises community-led response over top-down enforcement.

## How It Works

1. **Report** — A resident notices a safety concern and reports it via the meSafe platform (web or SMS).
2. **Route** — The report is automatically routed to the appropriate community responder or authority.
3. **Respond** — The responder acknowledges and acts on the report.
4. **Feedback** — The original reporter receives an update on what action was taken.
5. **Analyse** — Community leaders access aggregated safety data to inform planning.

## Early Results

In our pilot communities, we have observed:

- Increased willingness to report safety concerns when anonymity is guaranteed.
- Faster response times compared to traditional informal channels.
- Higher resident satisfaction with community safety management.

*Note: These are preliminary observations from our pilot phase. Full impact data will be published in our annual report.*

## Looking Ahead

We are expanding the meSafe programme to additional communities across the region. We are also working on:

- **Integration with local emergency services** where appropriate.
- **Community safety training** for volunteer responders.
- **Data partnerships** with research institutions to better understand safety patterns.
