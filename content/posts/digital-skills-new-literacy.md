## The Digital Divide Is a Skills Divide

Across sub-Saharan Africa, internet connectivity is growing faster than ever. Yet access alone does not guarantee participation. Millions of young people are graduating from secondary school and university with no practical experience in coding, data literacy, or artificial intelligence — the foundational skills of the modern economy.

At Orao Impact Foundation, we believe **digital skills are the new literacy**. Just as reading and writing unlocked opportunity for generations past, coding, AI basics, and digital fluency are the gateway to opportunity today.

> "The question is no longer whether Africa will digitise — it is whether Africa's youth will lead that digitisation or merely consume it." — Orao Impact Foundation

## What the Numbers Say

- Over **60% of Africa's population is under 25**, making it the world's youngest continent.
- The African digital economy is projected to reach **$180 billion by 2025**, yet local talent pipelines remain thin.
- Less than **3% of Nigerian students** currently receive any formal exposure to programming before university.

These are not just statistics — they represent real opportunity gaps that Orao Impact Foundation is working to close.

## The Orao Digital Empowerment Program

Our **Orao Digital Empowerment Program** is a hands-on, community-based training initiative targeting youth aged 16–30. The program covers:

- **Digital Literacy Fundamentals** — navigating the internet safely, using productivity tools, and understanding data privacy.
- **Introduction to Coding** — HTML, CSS, and basic Python through project-based learning.
- **AI Basics for Everyone** — understanding how AI works, practical use cases, and ethical considerations.
- **Entrepreneurship & Freelancing** — connecting skills to income through platforms and self-employment.

### Our Approach

We partner with community centres, secondary schools, and universities to deliver blended learning experiences. Participants receive mentorship from industry professionals and graduate with a portfolio of real projects.

## Why It Matters Now

The global economy is moving fast. Automation is reshaping traditional jobs while creating entirely new categories of work. African youth who are equipped with digital skills today will be the engineers, designers, and entrepreneurs shaping the continent tomorrow.

The window of opportunity is open — but it will not stay open forever.
